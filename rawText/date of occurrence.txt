(a) For civil cases (other than under insured incident 7 Tax protection), the date of the event which leads to a claim. If there is more than one event arising at different times from the same originating cause, the date of occurrence is the date of the first of these events. (This is the date the event happened, which may be before the date you first became aware of it.) (b) For criminal cases, the date you began, or are alleged to have begun, to break the law. (c) For insured incident 7 Tax protection, the date when the Revenue Commissioners first notify you in writing of their intention to carry out a revenue audit.
